Unmodeled Factors

The financial delta above understates the case for waiting. These qualitative factors would only widen the gap, but they are difficult to price and are therefore excluded from the primary calculation.

+Tailwinds for waiting
  • Q4 pricing pressure. October–November typically brings model-year turnover, end-of-year clearance, and fresher CPO inventory. A 2–3% price improvement on $35k pays for the entire rental spend.
  • Cash stays deployed. The $35k remains invested during the delay — additional yield the model already partially credits, but conservatively.
  • Negotiating leverage. No pressure to close, ability to walk away, and broader shortlist all improve terms.
  • No wasted miles. Every mile driven in the rental is a mile not accrued on the BMW's odometer.
  • Flexibility to hold out. If the right car isn't listed by Nov 20, the schedule tolerates a slip.
!Risks & watchouts
  • Rental availability. Requires re-booking Rental #2 with the same coupon or an equivalent rate. Book early.
  • Insurance verification. GEICO liability extension needs written confirmation before Rental #1 begins.
  • BMW price drop may not materialize. The base case is agnostic on this; if prices hold flat, the savings are still ~$759.
  • Rental price shock. Even a $10/day rate increase on Rental #2 preserves >$450 in savings.
  • Unforeseen need for a car during travel. Low probability; a one-off rental would cost <$100/day.
